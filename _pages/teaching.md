I am currently serving as a teaching assistant for both undergraduate and postgraduate statistics courses in the field of psychology. My role involves assisting with various aspects of course delivery, including:

1. **Classroom Instruction**: I support the primary lecturer by helping to clarify complex statistical concepts and methods, ensuring that students grasp the material effectively.

2. **Practical Sessions**: I conduct hands-on sessions in SPSS where students apply statistical techniques to real-world psychological data, reinforcing their understanding through practical application.

3. **Grading and Feedback**: I am responsible for grading assignments and exams, as well as providing detailed feedback to help students improve their skills and knowledge.

4. **Resource Development**: I contribute to the creation of educational materials, such as workshop slides, practice problems, and study guides, to support student learning.

5. **Research Support**: I assist students with their research projects, including advising on the appropriate statistical methods to use and helping with data analysis.

My goal is to create a supportive and engaging learning environment that fosters an understanding of statistical principles and their application in psychological research.
